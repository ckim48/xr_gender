    Project XR

    Gender Studies team

    The Gender Studies team for Project XR examined gender roles in society and explored new gender identities using Queer Theory, Intersectionality, and LGBTQ+ Rights and Identities as lenses. Additionally, the team investigated how these ideas can be integrated into virtual reality platforms while considering user engagement with such applications. The team's findings were presented in a research paper “Deconstructing a Traditional Gender Norm via VR Platform''. The research paper explores how VR environments extend our understanding of Gender Studies and the different theories associated with it.

    Reviewing virtual reality (VR) platforms using Queer Theory, Intersectionality and LGBTQ+ Rights and Identities.

    Deconstructing traditional gender norms by looking at how users react in a VR environment.

    Scrutinizing social-cultural portrayals of gender and sexuality for debunking usual gender role expectations through VR.

    Research Paper

    Deconstructing a Traditional Gender Norm via VR Platform

    "Deconstructing a Traditional Gender Norm via VR Platform" is an examination of how virtual reality (VR) can be utilized to question and break traditional gender roles. It looks at different gender theories such as inequality between genders, queer theory, intersectionality as well as LGBTQ rights and identities. The paper investigates a VR system that replicates many situations in order for people to see through other’s eyes in terms of gender thereby enhancing inclusivity and compassion. Additionally, the paper explores how this new method could change people’s perception about sex stratification in the society.

    Data Analysis

    Data Visualization

    Data visualization plays a crucial role in the XR - Gender Studies project by transforming complex data sets into clear and insightful graphical representations. Through the use of charts, graphs, and other visual tools, we can effectively communicate patterns, trends, and correlations within our research data. This not only enhances our understanding of gender-related phenomena but also makes the information accessible to a wider audience. By visualizing data, we can identify key insights, such as stress levels distribution among different genders or the relationship between stress and self-confidence. These visual tools enable us to convey our findings in a more engaging and comprehensible manner, ultimately supporting our goal of challenging traditional gender norms through immersive VR experiences.
